HEALTH MATTERS

Introduction:

 The human body serves as an automobile , basically being powererd by an engine and runs on fuel, glucose serves as the body fuel and the heart serves as the engine. The human body is similar to that of an automobile but more efficient, more complex and more durable. It incorporates several systems which are all interrelated and all depend on one and other for maximal efficiency. This website's basic function is to inform individuals about ways to improve their health. We will first discuss diseases according to age, first the diseases that commonly occur in people between the age of 40-50.

Common diseases that occur in people of age 40-50

  • STROKE:This is also known as Cerobrovascul Accident(CVA),this occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted, either by a blockage or a rupture to a blood vessel. This deprives brain cells of oxygen and nutrients, causing them to die and leading to potential long term disability or death . It is an acute brain damage manifesting with loss of consciousness,weakness of one side of the body or sudden loss of speech. Stroke usually complicates diabetes and hypertension and is thus a partly preventable condition through sensible nutritional choices. This complication is seen frequently in uncontrolled diabetes mellitus and hypertension and dietary modifications must be included in the Management. Low salt,low fat and weight reducing diets are therefore typically prescribed for most people who have suffered a stroke. Strokes and heart attacks have been linked to high cholesterol diets.Prevention focuses on managing risk factors through lifestyle changes and medical intervention

Ways of preventing stroke includes:

  • Healthy Diet
  • Regular Excercise
  • Weight Management
  • Quit Smoking
  • Limit Alcohol Consumption
  • Manage Stress
  • Control Blood Pressure
  • Manage Cholesterol

    • ARTHRITIS:This is an inflammation and degeneration in the joints. It is common in the middle aged and elderly who have excess weight. It causes pain and deformities that commonly occur in the elderly. When not qualified it refers to osteoarthritis which typically affects the weight bearing joints of the spine,knees and ankles. It can lead to great pain,deformities and disabilities of the limbs. It is commoner in the over nourished(obese) and in those who have high phosphate intake(bread,pastries,etc). Attempts to reduce damage by Arthritis include dietary supplements like glucosamine and chondrotin which many believe reduces degenerative processes(though this has not been clinically approved).

    4. Adopt a balanced diet
    5. Protect your joints
    • HYPERTENSION:It is believed that hypertension, like diabetes has a lot to do with diet. Research has shown that low salt diet is associated with reduced hypertension prevelance and vice versa. Foods rich in grains, fruit(which are high in potassium and low in sodium)lead to lower blood pressures over time. Over-nutrition(Overweight and obesity) is also closely related with hypertension and blood pressure has an almost linear relationship with weight. So the heavier you are,the higher the blood pressure you will likely have. Hypertension is possibly precipitated by excessive salt intake over the years, While gout(a medical condition that causes inflammation)has been linked to the consumption of excessive wine and protein. The main cause of hpertension is simply not one reason but a combination of genetic and lifestyle factor. .

    Simple Ways For Stopping Hypertension
    1. Focus on lifestyle changes
    2. Eating a healthy diet
    3. Doing regular excercise
    4. Limiting alcohol consumption
    5. Quit smoking
    • DIABETES:Diabetes Mellitus is a chronic(long standing) metabolic disorder in which blood sugars are abnormally high. This may be due to lack of or inefectiveness of insulin(the blood glucose reducing hormone produced by the pancreas). Diabetes mellitus(DM) is diagnosed by the findings of fasting blood sugar of greater than or equal to 126mg/dL(7.0mmo1/l ).The diagnosis may also be made by the findings of blood glucose taken at anytime except the fasting state of greater than or equal to 200mg/dL(11.1mmo1/L).High blood glucose often leads to passage of excess quantities of urine and excessive thirst and drinking of plenty of water daily. Quite often there may also be weight loss,malaise and blurred vision but in some people there are no symptoms. The main cause of diabetes is the inability of the body to produce or effectively use insulin.
    TYPES OF DIABETES
    1. Type 1 Diabetes mellitus- Symptoms include; Increased thirst, Frequent urination, Hunger, Fatigue. Most people with type 1 diabetes dont experience symptoms
    2. Type 2 Diabetes mellitus- symptoms include; Increased thirst, Frequent urination, Hunger, Fatigue.
    3. GDM(Gestational Diabetes Mellitus)
    4. IGT(Impaired Glucose Tolerance)
    5. Other types of diabetes mellitus

    NUTRIENT TYPES,CALORIC CONTENTS AND SOURCES SUITABLE FOR DIABETIC PATIENTS OR THOSE WHO ARE AVOIDING DIABETES

    GROUPS

  • Protein:This is important for muscles and bone growth. It provides 4 calories per gram. Protein is available from Meat and Plants. Foods high in protein include: Meat,Milk,Fish,Chicken,Turkey,Egg white and Beans . Animal sources provide protein of higher biological value,compared with plant sources. protein intake should make up to 15-20% of total caloric intake.
    • See more

  • Carbohydrate:In people with DM(Diabetes mellitus) as well as those without DM, carbohydrate should make up to 60-70% of the diet. This is the main energy source for the body EXAMPLES OF SIMPLE CARBOHYDRATES:Bread, Cereals-cornflakes,Crackers,Fruits,Baked Beans,Fried potatoes,Yam,Rice. WHO recommends that all people(not only diabetic) should limit their refined or table sugar intake to less than 10 percent of calories.Starchy carbohydrates and sugar have comparable absorption rates. Many people eat beans as a way of controlling their blood sugar . This is wrong as beans has both Protein and Carbohydrate but cannot take the place of a balanced healthy diet. Complex carbohydrates are better than the simple carbohydrates.

  • Fat:This is an important source of energy and it is calorie dense. It is recommended that it makes up 20-35% of the total calorie intake. It is slowly digested and contributes to satiety. It provides 9 calories per gram hance it is more likely to lead to weight gain. The main fats in the body are divided int four:monosaturated,polyunsaturated(PUFA),Saturated and trans-fats. The unsaturated fats are healthier and prevent heart disease. These are found in olive oil,coconut oil,vegetable oils. The saturated and trans-fats include most animal fats like the fats in Turkey,Beef,Pork and Chicken. Palm oil is a healthy saturated vegetable oil. To reduce the fat content of the diet, remove the skin from chicken/turkey and avoid buying meats which have visible fats. The softer or more liquid a fat is, the less saturated it.

  • Vitamins and Minerals:These are micronutrients important for growth, formation of blood cells, healthy skin, good vision, strong bones and teeth. Generally people who eat a well balanced diet do not need vitamin supplements which are readily available over the counter. There are major minerals like Calcium,Phosphorus,Sodium,Chloride,Magnesium,Potassium,Sulphur while the minor or trace minerals are Cobalt,Copper,Iron,Manganese,Selenium and Zinc. Some vitamins have minerals they work with for instance calcium and vitamin D,Selenium and vitamin E. Their deficiency or excess can lead to disease.

  • Sources of vitamins and minerals:Fruits and vegetables. Most cereals are fortified with Calcium. High salt can be found in tinned sardines,pickled food,most fast foods,"suya" and local "pepper soup" preperations from Nigeria. Salt intake for all people should be less than 2,300mg (i.e less than 1 teaspoonful) per day.

  • Water:This is the most important nutrient for the survival of humans. Water serves as a coolant and a waste remover . Two thirds of the human body is made up of water. At least 8 glasses(2.5 litres) of water need to be drunk daily. The amount may be higher in hot dry climates due to greater water loss.

  • Fiber:Fiber is not a seperate class of nutrients but is very important and is found incorporated in the energy nutrients. Dietary fiber is the part of plants that does not get digested nor absorbed. Fiber is essential for better functioning of the digestive system and does not lead to weight gain. There are two types of fiber. They are water soluble and water insoluble fiber. Fiber can be found in fruits,cereals(oats,apples,oranges,wheat) and vegetables . These are helpful in preventing constipation and help in reducing cholesterol levels. Adequate fiber intake is 15-20 grams per 1000 calories or 25-50 grams per day which can be found in two to three servings of fruits and vegetables.
